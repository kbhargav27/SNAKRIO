# SNAKRIO Snack Brand – Easy Startup Guide (India)

This document provides a **simple step-by-step guide to start a ready-to-eat snack brand** such as **chips, makhana, or namkeen** under the brand name **SNAKRIO**.

This guide is designed for **individual founders starting alone with a small budget**.

---

# 1. Choose Business Structure

Start your business as: Sole Proprietorship

### Why this is recommended
- Very easy to start
- Lowest cost
- Minimal compliance
- No complex legal setup

You can later convert the business to **LLP or Private Limited** if the brand grows.

---

# 2. Register Your Brand Name (Trademark)

Register the brand name: SNAKRIO

### Trademark Category Class 30

This class includes:

- Namkeen
- Chips
- Roasted makhana
- Ready-to-eat snacks
- Biscuits
- Spices
- Bakery products

### Government Authority
Trademark registration is handled by the  
Controller General of Patents, Designs and Trade Marks (India).

### Cost ₹4500 (Individual)

After filing the application you can start using: SNAKRIO™

---

# 3. Obtain FSSAI Registration

Every food business must obtain a license from the  
Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI).

### Start with Basic FSSAI Registration

### Cost ₹100 per year

### Required Documents

- Aadhaar Card
- Passport-size photo
- Business address proof
- Mobile number and email

### Official Portal https://foscos.fssai.gov.in/

---

# 4. Open a Business Bank Account

Open a **Current Account** in the business name.

Example business name: SNAKRIO FOODS

### Documents Required

- Aadhaar card
- PAN card
- FSSAI registration
- Business details

Common banks used by startups:

- State Bank of India
- HDFC Bank
- ICICI Bank

---

# 5. Register MSME (Udyam)

Register your business under **MSME (Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises)**.

### Benefits

- Government subsidies
- Easier loans
- Machinery support schemes
- Business credibility

### Registration Portal https://udyamregistration.gov.in

### Cost Free

---

# 6. GST Registration (Optional Initially)

GST is required if:

- Annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakh
- Selling on online platforms
- Supplying large retailers

### Portal https://gst.gov.in

### Cost Free (if you apply yourself)

---

# 7. Setup Small Snack Production

You can start with a **small production unit or home kitchen**.

### Basic Equipment Required

- Roasting pan or fryer
- Mixing bowl or mixing machine
- Weighing scale
- Pouch sealing machine
- Storage containers

### Estimated Setup Cost ₹30,000 – ₹80,000

---

# 8. Packaging Requirements

Every snack packet must include the following details:

- Brand name
- FSSAI license number
- Net weight
- Ingredient list
- Manufacturing date
- Expiry / Best before date
- Maximum Retail Price (MRP)
- Manufacturer address
- Customer care number

### Recommended Packet Sizes 20g, 50g, 100g

These sizes sell best for snacks.

---

# 9. Start Selling Your Products

## Offline Sales

Start with local distribution:

- Kirana stores
- Local grocery shops
- Supermarkets
- Small distributors

## Online Sales (Later Stage)

You can later sell on quick-commerce and delivery platforms such as:

- Zepto
- Swiggy Instamart
- Blinkit

These platforms usually require:

- FSSAI license
- GST registration
- Proper packaging

---

# 10. Minimum Startup Budget

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|-----|----------------|
| Trademark Registration | ₹4500 |
| FSSAI Registration | ₹100 |
| Packaging Design & Printing | ₹10,000 |
| Machines / Equipment | ₹30,000 – ₹50,000 |
| Raw Materials | ₹10,000 |

### Total Estimated Budget ₹50,000 – ₹80,000

---

# 3-Month Startup Plan

## Month 1
- Choose brand name
- Apply for trademark
- Apply for FSSAI registration
- Design packaging

## Month 2
- Purchase machines
- Buy raw materials
- Start small production
- Approach local shops

## Month 3
- Increase production
- Expand distribution
- Apply for GST
- Approach online platforms

---

# Conclusion

Starting a snack brand like **SNAKRIO** is possible with a small investment and simple registrations.

The recommended order:
1. Sole Proprietorship
2. Trademark Registration
3. FSSAI License
4. Business Bank Account
5. MSME Registration
6. GST Registration (if needed)
7. Production Setup
8. Start Selling

By starting small and gradually expanding, you can build a scalable snack brand.

---

# Future Growth Ideas

Once the brand grows you can:

- Expand to more snack varieties
- Partner with distributors
- Sell on quick-commerce platforms
- Convert the business to Private Limited
- Launch your own website and online store
