# Trademark Registration Guide for Snack Brand (SNAKRIO)

This guide explains the **documents required and step-by-step process to register a trademark in India** for your snack brand (e.g., **SNAKRIO**).

Trademark registration protects your **brand name, logo, and identity** so that no one else can legally use a similar name in the same category.

# 1. What Can Be Registered as a Trademark

You can register:

- Brand name (e.g., **SNAKRIO**)
- Logo
- Tagline / slogan
- Product name
- Packaging design (in some cases)

For a snack brand, the most common registrations are:

- **Word mark** (brand name)
- **Logo mark**

---

# 2. Trademark Class for Snack Products

Every trademark must be registered under a specific **class**.

For packaged snacks, the common class is:

| Class | Category |
|------|-----------|
| 30 | Snacks, namkeen, chips, makhana, food products |

Example products in Class 30:
- Chips
- Namkeen
- Makhana
- Ready-to-eat snacks
- Biscuits
- Spices

# 3. Documents Required for Trademark Registration

## 1. Applicant Identity Proof
Any one of the following:

- Aadhaar Card
- PAN Card
- Passport

## 2. Address Proof
Examples:

- Electricity bill
- Driving license
- Aadhaar card

## 3. Brand Name or Logo
You need to provide:

- Brand name (SNAKRIO)
- Logo file (JPEG or PNG)

## 4. Business Proof (optional but recommended)

If you have a business entity:

- GST certificate
- Udyam/MSME certificate
- Partnership deed (if applicable)

## 5. Power of Attorney (Form TM-48)
Required if filing through a **trademark attorney or agent**.

---

# 4. Trademark Registration Process

## Step 1 – Trademark Search

Check if your brand name is already registered.

Search on the official trademark portal: https://ipindiaonline.gov.in/trademarkefiling

Look for similar names in **Class 30**.

---

## Step 2 – Prepare Application

Prepare the application with:

- Brand name or logo
- Owner details
- Business category
- Trademark class (Class 30)

---

## Step 3 – File Trademark Application

Apply through the official website: https://ipindia.gov.in/

Submit **Form TM-A**.

After submission, you receive a **Trademark Application Number**.

You can now use: ™ (TM symbol)


with your brand name.

Example: SNAKRIO™


---

## Step 4 – Examination by Trademark Office

The trademark office reviews your application.

Possible outcomes:

- Accepted
- Objection raised
- Request for clarification

If there is an objection, you must submit a **reply**.

---

## Step 5 – Trademark Journal Publication

If accepted, your trademark is published in the **Trademark Journal**.

Public gets **4 months to oppose** the trademark.

---

## Step 6 – Registration Certificate

If no opposition is filed:

You receive the **Trademark Registration Certificate**.

Now you can use the **® symbol**.

Example: SNAKRIO®

---

# 6. Time Required

Typical timeline:

| Stage | Time |
|------|------|
| Application filing | 1 day |
| Examination | 3–6 months |
| Journal publication | 4 months |
| Final registration | 8–18 months |

However, you can **start using the brand immediately with ™** after filing.

---

# 7. Trademark Validity

Trademark validity: 10 years

It can be **renewed every 10 years**.

---

# 8. Benefits of Trademark Registration

- Legal protection for your brand
- Prevents others from copying your brand name
- Builds trust with customers
- Required for many e-commerce platforms
- Adds value to your business
